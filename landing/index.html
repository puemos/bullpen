What it actually is

A workbench, not a wrapper.

Bullpen starts your ACP agent and hands it a bullpen-analysis MCP server with typed submission tools. The agent can research however it wants — but it has to file its findings through those tools.

A run can’t finalize without a thesis, a risks block, at least one source, and a stance. No wall of markdown. No hand-waving. If the agent won’t commit, the report doesn’t close.

Reports live in local SQLite. No account. No cloud sync. The only network call is your agent’s own.

The report

What you actually get back.

Why not just paste it into a chat.

Four things you don’t get from a chat window.

01 · Evidence

Every claim has a source

Every number, quote, and call goes through a typed MCP tool with a source attached. Click to see the original.

02 · Stance

The agent has to commit

Bullish, bearish, mixed, or neutral — with confidence. The run won’t finalize without one. No “on one hand, on the other.”

03 · Scenarios

Three scenarios, side by side

Base, upside, downside. The agent has to write all three. You see the range, not one confident-sounding guess.

04 · Private

Stays on your computer

Local SQLite. No account, no telemetry, no cloud sync. Your agent’s auth is the only network call.

The four calls

Bullish — points up Bearish — points down Mixed — it’s contested Neutral — not enough to call it

How it works

Three steps. Then you read.

Step 01

Ask a question

Plain English. “Compare NVDA to AMD.” “Is the energy sector overbought?” Whatever you’d ask someone who actually reads filings.

Step 02

Pick your agent

Bullpen detects the ACP agents on your machine — Claude Code, Codex, Gemini, OpenCode, or your own via BULLPEN_CUSTOM_AGENT. It uses the agent’s own auth.

Step 03

Read the report

Thesis, key numbers, risks, three scenarios, a stance. Saved to local SQLite. Come back to it whenever.
